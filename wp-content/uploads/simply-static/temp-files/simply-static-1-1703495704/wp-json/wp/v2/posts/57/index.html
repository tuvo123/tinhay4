Terror filled the heart of a World War I soldier when he saw his lifelong friend fall in battle.
\nTrapped in a trench and artillery shells flying continuously overhead, the soldier asked his commander to allow him to go out into the “plain area” between the trenches to bring back his wounded comrade.<\/p>\n

The commander said:<\/p>\n

– You can go but I think the job won’t be worth it. Perhaps his friend was dead and he could lose his own life.<\/p>\n

Regardless of the commander’s words, the soldier still left. Miraculously, he managed to reach his friend, lifted him onto his shoulders, and carried him back to their trench. As they both tumbled into the trench, the commander checked on the hit soldier and then looked sympathetically at his friend.<\/p>\n

-I told you, that job is not worth it. – The commander said – Your friend is dead, and you are seriously injured.<\/p>\n

The soldier replied:<\/p>\n

– Even so, the job is still worth doing, boss.<\/p>\n

– What do you mean by worth doing? Your friend is dead, right?<\/p>\n

-Boss, it was worth doing because when I got to him, he was still alive and I was very satisfied when he told me “Jim, I knew that you would definitely come to me!”<\/p>\n

In life, whether something is worth doing or not depends entirely on how we look at it. Be courageous and do what your heart tells you so that later in life you won’t have to regret not doing it.
